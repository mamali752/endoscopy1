Endoscopy

  • An endoscopy is a test to look inside your body.

  • long, thin tube with a small camera inside, called an endoscope, is
    passed into your body through a natural opening such as your mouth.

  • Your GP may refer you for an endoscopy if you're having certain symptoms.
    It will usually be done at an endoscopy unit in a hospital.

  • There are different types of endoscopy that look at different
    parts of the body.

  • The type of endoscopy you have will depend on your symptoms.

Types of endoscopy

  1. colonoscopy: in your bottom to check your bowels

  2. colposcopy in your vagina to check your cervix (the opening to your womb)

  3. Cystoscopy in your urethra (tube where pee comes out) to check your bladder

  4. Gastroscopy in your mouth to check your oesophagus (food pipe),
    stomach and part of the small intestine

  5. Bronchoscopy is a procedure to look directly at the airways in
    the lungs using a thin, lighted tube
